FAQ

 

How long is therapy?

This depends on your presenting problems, but, usually, no less than 8 sessions. Some clients may require or choose long-term therapy for ongoing problems, and other clients are able to address targeted issues within 8-10 sessions. It all depends on your needs, and this will be discussed and revisited throughout treatment.

 

What can I expect? 

A safe, non-judgmental environment to discuss what your problems are, how they are affecting you, and what changes you would like to make. Therapy is NOT advice. It is a space where we will identify patterns, help connect dots, process difficult emotions and experiences, and identify tools that may be more helpful than current ways of coping. 
 

Are you faith-based?

I am a Christian, and incorporate faith and spirituality into therapy when my clients request to approach therapy from that worldview. The modalities I use for therapy do not require a Christian or faith-based approach, and I always consider and respect the spiritual beliefs of my clients. I have worked with clients from many backgrounds, and only bring faith/spiritual beliefs into the therapeutic space when the client invites it.
 

How do I get started?

I offer a free 15-minute consultation, so you can simply call, text, or email to schedule a no-obligation phone call to see if I'm the right fit for you.
 

How do I know who the right therapist is for me?

Ask yourself the following: Does the therapist list the issues you are struggling with? Does the therapist feel like someone you could be open with in time? Are you okay with their gender, approach to therapy, and mode of therapy (in-person or virtual)?
 

Do you take insurance?

No, I do not. I do work with lien agreements and offer an income-based sliding scale. 
 

How much does this cost?

My fee per session is $200, unless a lower fee is agreed upon, based on your income. If you are a grad psych student, I charge $100 per session. 
 

Do you have availability?

Yes! I currently have availability during the week-days.
