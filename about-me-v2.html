About Me

 

My passion for helping others started at a very young age. I always knew I was going to be a helper of some kind. After deciding on psychology, I went to Vanguard University of Southern California, where I received my Bachelor’s degree in psychology in 2010. I then went on to earn my Master’s of Science in Clinical Psychology in 2015 at Vanguard as well. Even while earning my degrees, I began to work in the mental health field. I also started my own journey in therapy, and felt and saw what a difference insight and healing makes. Then, as I started working towards my hours needed for my license of Marriage and Family Therapy, I dove into the field, and never looked back!

 
In September of 2024, I decided to open my own practice. After 6 years of licensed experience, 10 years of working as a therapist, and over 12 years of working with those struggling with their mental health, I decided to use my experience in a way that is also sustainable for my health. Each and every role and position I have had the opportunity to be in has taught me so much. About mental health, this field, and myself.
 
I am incredibly grateful and excited to be able to get back to direct patient care and see clients one-on-one, in a space where I’ve seen so much healing take place. This work is so rewarding and I consider it a privilege to do this work.
